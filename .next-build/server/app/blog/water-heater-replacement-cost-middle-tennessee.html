What Does Water Heater Replacement Cost in Middle Tennessee in 2026? (Real Prices)

Middle Tennessee water heater replacement prices in 2026 — tank vs. tankless, brand by brand, what's included, and where the surprise charges hide.

AK

Aaron Kelly

Licensed Master Plumber, Honest Plumbing

Average water heater replacement cost in Middle Tennessee (2026)

After surveying pricing across roughly 25 Middle Tennessee plumbing companies and reviewing our own 2025–2026 invoices, here's the real picture. A standard 40–50 gallon gas tank water heater fully installed in the Middle Tennessee runs $1,400–$2,800 — that includes the unit, labor, permit, haul-away of the old heater, expansion tank if required by code, and warranty registration. Electric tank installs run a bit less ($1,200–$2,400) because no venting work is needed. Tankless conversions are $3,500–$6,500 installed, with the spread driven mostly by whether the existing gas line needs upsizing and whether new venting has to be run. These are real numbers, not bait pricing — anything advertised dramatically lower usually leaves significant work off the quote.

What separates a $1,400 install from a $2,800 install in Middle Tennessee

Honest answer: usually three things. First, the unit itself. A bottom-tier 40-gallon AO Smith from a big-box store costs $475–$575 retail; a Bradford White Defender 50-gallon (only available through licensed plumbers) is $850–$1,100 wholesale. Both work; the Bradford has a noticeably lower warranty-claim rate based on our 5+ years of failure data, and gets installed in more of our higher-end Brentwood, Nolensville, and Brentwood jobs. Second, the actual scope of work. A clean swap with existing water shutoffs and venting in good shape is a 2–3 hour job; an install requiring new ball valves, an expansion tank, replacement of a corroded gas flex line, and re-venting can run 5–7 hours. Third, who's doing the work — a Tennessee-licensed master plumber with permits pulled and inspection scheduled is different from a handyman with a pickup truck.

Why we install Bradford White as our standard recommendation

Bradford White builds tank water heaters in Middleville, Michigan, and sells them exclusively through licensed plumbing wholesalers — you can't pick one up at a big-box store. Across our own warranty claim records from the last several years, Bradford has the lowest premature failure rate of any tank brand we install, and the warranty parts pipeline is fast (most parts ship same-day from regional warehouses). We install AO Smith and State Industries (made in Ashland City, TN) as well, especially for budget-driven jobs and warranty work — both are fine — but Bradford is what we install in our own homes.

Tank vs. tankless: when each makes financial sense in Middle Tennessee

Tankless math in Middle Tennessee: a typical natural-gas tankless saves $80–$140/year over a tank unit, gives you 18–22 years of service life (vs. 10–13 for a tank), and recovers basement floor space. The upfront cost difference is $2,000–$4,500. Payback math depends on (a) how long you'll live in the house, and (b) whether you currently run out of hot water at peak use. If you'll be there 10+ years and the tank can't keep up, tankless almost always pencils. For short-term homeowners or households with adequate tank capacity, a tank replacement at $1,400–$2,800 is the better economic choice. We do the actual payback calculation during the quote — we don't push tankless when it doesn't make sense for you.

Hidden charges that show up on competitor quotes (but shouldn't)

When you compare Middle Tennessee water heater installation quotes, check what's actually included. Things that should be in a flat-price install quote: permit, haul-away of the old heater, expansion tank (required by Metro code on closed systems), new water shutoff valves if existing ones are corroded, gas flex line replacement if needed, warranty registration, and the post-install inspection. Things that frequently show up as surprise add-ons mid-job at less reputable companies: "premium installation" upcharges, code-required parts charged separately, disposal fees, and "emergency service" surcharges that get added after the truck arrives. Our flat-price quote includes everything; if we find a surprise after the truck arrives, we tell you the cost in advance and you decide whether to proceed.

How long does it take to replace a water heater in Middle Tennessee?

Standard same-size, same-fuel-type tank replacement: 2–4 hours from truck arrival to clean-up. Tank-to-tankless conversion: 6–10 hours (sometimes split across two days if extensive gas line or venting work is required). Commercial water heater replacements: 4–12 hours depending on size and venting. Most residential calls in the Middle Tennessee are scheduled same-day or next-day from the time you call, assuming we have your model in stock at our warehouse on Murfreesboro shop.

Warranty coverage on water heater installations

We're authorized warranty service for AO Smith, Bradford White, State Industries, and Whirlpool. Standard manufacturer warranty on tank water heaters is 6–12 years on the tank and 1 year on parts; we register your warranty with the manufacturer at install (most homeowners never do this themselves) so coverage is in place from day one. We also include a 1-year labor warranty on our installation work. If a unit we installed fails during the warranty period, we handle the warranty parts claim and the re-install — you don't deal with the manufacturer.

How to choose: the three questions to ask before booking

Before booking any Middle Tennessee water heater installation, ask three things. (1) Is the quote flat-price, and what specifically is included — permit, haul-away, expansion tank, code-required parts, warranty registration? Get a written list, not a verbal "yeah, that's all in there." (2) Who is actually doing the install, and are they working under a Tennessee-licensed master plumber? (Aaron Kelly is the master plumber on every Honest Plumbing job.) (3) What's the labor warranty on the install? One year is the industry standard for reputable companies; "no labor warranty" is a red flag. The right Middle Tennessee plumber for you is the one whose pricing structure, license, and warranty match what's actually being installed in your home.

Quick answers

Frequently asked.

Is more expensive water heater installation actually better in Middle Tennessee?
Sometimes, but not linearly. The jump from a $1,400 bottom-bid install to a $2,000–$2,500 install typically buys real value — better brand, properly pulled permit, code-required parts included, and 1-year labor warranty. The jump from $2,500 to $3,500+ on a standard tank install usually doesn't buy meaningfully more — that's typically inflated pricing or unnecessary add-ons. Tankless conversions legitimately cost more because the scope of work is genuinely larger.
Do I need a permit to replace a water heater in Middle Tennessee?
Yes. local code Codes requires a plumbing permit for water heater replacement, and the install must pass inspection. Reputable Middle Tennessee plumbers (including us) pull the permit, include the cost in the quote, and coordinate the Metro inspection. Anyone offering to skip the permit to save you $75 is also skipping the inspection that protects you if something goes wrong.
How long does a water heater installation take in Middle Tennessee?
Standard tank-to-tank same-fuel replacement: 2–4 hours. Tank-to-tankless conversion: 6–10 hours (sometimes spread over two days for extensive gas line or venting work). Same-day service is typical for the standard call when scheduled before mid-morning.
What brands of water heater do you install in Middle Tennessee?
We install Bradford White (our default recommendation), AO Smith, State Industries (made in Ashland City, TN), Rheem, and Whirlpool. For tankless: Rinnai, Navien, Noritz, and Rheem. We're authorized warranty service for AO Smith, Bradford White, State, and Whirlpool, so warranty parts and labor are streamlined when those units fail under warranty.
